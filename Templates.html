Welcome to OysterFlows! This app is designed to give you the opportunity to simulate decisions on water use upstream and see the effect on oyster health downstream.

We use flow estimates, salinity information, and the biology of the oyster to calculate an “eco-flows analysis.” It is currently available for the Sabine and Calcasieu River basins in Louisiana.

The Sabine River forms the border between Louisiana and Texas and flows into Sabine Lake, which contains the largest remaining natural oyster reef in the Gulf of Mexico. The river provides water to each state for drinking, farming and industrial uses. Dam release scenarios estimate the downstream effects of transferring water out of the Sabine River by controlling 3 inputs: the flow out of Toledo Bend Dam, the flow of the Neches River and sea level at Sabine Pass. By changing these inputs, you can control the balance of fresh and salt water in Sabine Lake and the effect of that new balance on salinity and, consequently, oyster health.


Sabine Lake Impacted by Dam Releases

The Calcasieu River begins in Vernon Parish, Louisiana and flows about 200 miles into Calcasieu Lake at the Gulf of Mexico coast where oyster reefs are harvested every year. On its way the Calcasieu River flows through Lake Charles, where its waters are used heavily for industrial and municipal purposes. Water withdrawal scenarios estimate the effects of that water removal on salinity and, consequently, oyster health, each month of the year in Calcasieu Lake.
